# Eligibility Codebook

## Core criterion
A paper is in scope if it **substantially** addresses criminal infiltration in the legal economy, including criminal control, influence, penetration, ownership, collusion, corruption, intimidation, laundering, or strategic presence in legal firms/markets/sectors/procurement/governance/ownership structures/apparently legitimate economic activity.

## Decision categories
- `eligible_core`: direct and central analysis of criminal infiltration in legal economy contexts.
- `eligible_contextual`: relevant but partial/indirect focus; still informative for mechanisms/sectors.
- `maybe_full_text_needed`: abstract/title insufficient; full-text screening required.
- `not_eligible`: topic does not meet criterion.
- `duplicate`: duplicate canonical record.
- `not_academic`: non-scholarly item outside review scope.
- `not_retrievable`: cannot retrieve enough content for assessment.

## Standardised exclusion reason codes
- `TOPIC_OFF_SCOPE`
- `NO_LEGAL_ECONOMY_LINK`
- `MENTION_ONLY_NOT_ANALYTICAL`
- `CRIME_DOMAIN_MISMATCH`
- `NO_EVIDENCE_ON_MECHANISM`
- `DOCUMENT_TYPE_EXCLUDED`
- `LANGUAGE_EXCLUDED`
- `DUPLICATE_RECORD`
- `NOT_ACADEMIC_SOURCE`
- `FULL_TEXT_UNAVAILABLE`

## Examples
- `eligible_core`: empirical study on mafia control of public procurement firms.
- `eligible_contextual`: corruption network paper with explicit section on criminal market penetration.
- `not_eligible` + `NO_LEGAL_ECONOMY_LINK`: organized crime violence paper with no legal-market interface.
- `maybe_full_text_needed`: abstract references infiltration but lacks sector/mechanism details.
